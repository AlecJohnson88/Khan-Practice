Brisket

  • Time: 17 hours
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Brisket 1
Salt Generous Coating
Pepper 2 Parts to every part salt

Step 1: Start fire in pit and bring up to temperature of 250 degrees.

Step 2: Trim and season brisket while pit heats up to proper temperature. Allow brisket to warm to room temperature.

Step 3: Place brisket with fat cap up and the point facing toward the fire box.

Step 4: Maintain temperature and take temperature after several hours. When brisket reaches an internal temprature of 165 degrees, remove from pit, wrap in butcher paper and return to pit with fat cap facing up.

Step 5: Check internal temprature of brisket and remove when it reaches a temperature of 200-203 degrees. Let rest for 1 hour before slicing against the grain and serving.

Source: Franklin BBQ

Pork Butt

  • Time: 24 Hours
  • Serves: 10
Ingredients Quantity
Boston Butt (Pork) 1 - Approx. 10-12 lbs
Butter 1 Stick
Sweet Rub Generous Coating (approx. 1 cup)

Step 1: Approximately 12-24 before planned cook time, fill large bowl with water and a large sprinkle of salt. Mix water and salt until salt disappears. Add pork butt to water ensuring that the water covers the meat completely. Cover in plastic wrap and place in fridge.

Step 2: Bring BBQ pit up to temperature of 225-250 degrees. While pit is warming, remove pork butt from brine and wash completely under cool water. Pat dry and set aside. Melt stick of butter in microwave and inject melted butter throughout pork butt. Sprinkle sweet rub over entire pork butt and cover generously.

Step 3: Place pork butt in pit and maintain temperature. After a few hours, check internal temperature. When internal temp reaches 165 degrees, wrap in aluminum foil with shiney side facing inward.

Step 4: Continue to monitor internal temperature. Remove from pit when internal temperature reaches 200 degrees. Let rest for 1 hour in foil.

Step 5: Shred with fork and serve on buns.

Source: Amazing Ribs

Pork Spare Ribs

  • Time: 5 Hours
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Spare Ribs 1 Rack
Sweet Rub Approx. 1/2 Cup

Step 1: Bring pit to temperature of 225-250 degrees

Step 2: While pit warms, remove ribs from packaging, pat dry, and trim off any extra fat or small bones.

Step 3: When pit is at correct temperature, place ribs on middle of rack with bone side down.

Step 4: Maintain temperature until ribs are dark on outside and the meat has drawn up on the bones. To test if meat is done, twist bones gently. If the bones can twist in the meat, the ribs are done and ready to be removed.

Step 5: Allow meat to rest for 15 minutes. Cut in between bones and serve.
